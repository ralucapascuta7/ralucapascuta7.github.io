About
I don't wait to be assigned problems. I find them.

My passion for building started early. During high school, I joined the DpIT Academy (Discover Your Passion in IT) where I created FlyBox — an emergency delivery solution designed to attach to any commercially-available drone capable of carrying at least 300g. That project taught me that good software solves real problems for real people.

In my first year at UBB, I started working on another project, teaming up with two students from UTCN to build Nabu - a personal AI therapist exploring how technology can support mental health. Together we competed in two competitions and I wrote a research paper about the project, which I presented at a student academic conference. Working across two universities taught me that the best solutions come from people who think differently.

The summer between my first and second year took me to the US on a Work & Travel program, where I sharpened my English, learned to navigate an entirely different culture, adapt fast, work in international teams, and be comfortable with uncertainty. It's the kind of experience that changes how you think, not just how you communicate.
